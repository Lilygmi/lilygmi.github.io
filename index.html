Climate Science, Ocean Ecology, and Carbon Cycling Research

I am a PhD student in Earth and Environmental Sciences at the University of Pennsylvania studying ocean ecology and biogeochemistry in climate models. My work focuses on understanding how marine ecosystems and carbon cycling respond to climate change, particularly in the Southern Ocean.

View Projects GitHub

About

My research combines climate modeling, ocean biogeochemistry, and ecological analysis to better understand how changing ocean conditions affect marine ecosystems and the global carbon cycle. I am particularly interested in Southern Ocean ecosystem dynamics, phytoplankton community structure, and the role of the ocean in climate mitigation.


Beyond research, I am passionate about science communication, environmental policy, and connecting scientific research to public decision-making.

Research Interests

Ocean Biogeochemistry

Investigating marine carbon cycling and nutrient dynamics within Earth system models.

Climate Modeling

Using CMIP6 climate models and machine learning methods to analyze ecosystem responses under climate change.

Southern Ocean Ecology

Studying phytoplankton dynamics and ecological shifts in rapidly changing polar systems.

Projects

CMIP6 Carbon Cycle Analysis

Evaluating ocean carbon uptake and ecosystem variability across coupled climate models.

Machine Learning for Ocean Ecology

Applying generalized additive models and neural networks to understand ecological drivers in marine systems.

Science Communication & Policy

Writing and advocacy focused on climate science funding, environmental justice, and marine-based climate solutions.
